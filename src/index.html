Col. David Hackworth

The colonel who went against the Vietnam War.

Hackworth in Vietnam.

A Timeline of Col. Hackworth's Life

Early life

  • 1930: Hackworth is born in Ocean Park, California.
  • 1931: Both his parents die. Him and his siblings are raised by his paternal grandmother.

Military Career

    Post-WW2

  • 1944: Joins the Merchant Marines, but sees no combat.
  • 1946: Enlists in the US army at age 16. After training, he gets deployed to Trieste, Italy, for postwar occuation duty.

    • Korean War

  • 1951: During the Korean War, he earns a battlefield commission as a second lieutenant.
  • 1951: Gets another battlefield commission to first lieutenant after a successful as assault on Hill 1062.
  • 1951: creates and commands the 27th Wolfhound Raiders, an entirely volunteer unit.
  • 1952: Returns to Korea on a second tour of duty, this time with the 40th Infantry Division. Gets a promotion to Captain.

    • Interwar Service

  • After the Korean War, Hackworth leaves the Army to go to college.
  • 1956: Gets bored with civilian life after two years of college. Re-enters the Army as a Captain.
  • Gets assigned initially to California, then to Germany.
  • 1961: Involved in several fire drills during the Berlin Crisis.
  • 1964: Graduates from Austin Peay State University with a bachelor's of science in history.

    Vietnam War

  • 1965: Deploys to Vietnam as a major. Serves as an operations officer and battalion commander in the 101st Airborne Division.
  • 1965: Founds the Tiger Force unit. Develops a reputation very quickly.
  • 1968-69: Assigned to a training battalion at Fort Lewis, Washington, and then returned to Vietnam to lead the 9th Infanty Division.
  • 1970: serves as a senior military advisor to the South Vietnamese.
  • 1971: promotion to colonel.
  • 1971: does an interview with ABC in which he strongly criticizes the Vietnam war and the army officials that are running it. This enrages the military, and he retires as a colonel.

Business Activity

  • 1970s: after leaving the army, he settles on the Australian Gold Coast, near Brisbane. He makes a fortune through profitable ventures that included real estate investing, a duck farm, and a popular restaurant called Scaramouche. He is also active in the Australian antinuclear movement.

Writing Career

  • Mid-1980s: Returns to the US, begins working as a contributing editor on defense issues for Newsweek. Also made various tv appearances to talk about the shortcomings of the military. He also helps veterans with PTSD, since he himself had dealt with it from both wars.
  • Mid-1990s: investigates a Navy Admiral for allegedly wearing invalid service medals.
  • 1996: goes to a combat zone in Bosnia and Herzegovina. He interviews several enlisted soldiers and officers. He is well known and respected throughout the military

Death

  • 2005: Hackworth dies of cancer in Tijuana, Mexico. He was 74 years old. The cancer was likely caused by Agent Blue, a chemical agent not as well known as Agent Orange, but deadly nonetheless.

Military Awards and Decorations

  • 2 Distinguished Service Crosses.
  • 10 Silver Stars.
  • 4 Legion of Merit.
  • Distinguished Flying Cross.
  • 8 Bronze Star Medals.
  • 8 Purple Hearts.
  • 34 Air Medals.
  • 4 Army Commendation Medals.
